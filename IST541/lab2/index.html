How to Make Job Aids

By Eric Li


Step 1: Define your Audience

Understanding who the job aid is meant for is key to creating a good job aid.

Survey the intended users, and get a grasp of their skillsets and general workflow.

announcement Pay attention to how they communicate, and what communication strategies are useful for them.

Image by Rudzhan. Image licensed under Adobe Stock Education License



Step 2: Define the Job

To create a useful Job Aid, you need to be familiar with the job.

Ask the audience about the particulars of the job. Pay attention to any information they deem important, or information they feel would be supplemental to their work.

announcement A Job Aid needs to be concise; eliminate information that is irrelevant, or something the audience is already very familiar with.

Image by tinyt.studio. Image licensed under Adobe Stock Education License



Step 3: Choose a Job Aid Format

A Job Aid's presentation should be tailored to convey the information efficiently.

Consider what visual format would fit in the workflow for your audience. Would having images help or not?

Some examples of formats:

  1. Flowchart
  2. Checklist
  3. Reference Guide
  4. Step by Step Instructions

Step 4: Test the Job Aid

You should send the finished Job Aid to a select group of employees for testing.

Consider sending the Job Aid to a mix of employees to gather effective feedback.

Refine the Job Aid according to feedback, such as removing unnecessary information or highlighting missed details

Image by alexdndz. Image licensed under Adobe Stock Education License



Step 5: Implement the Job Aid

When the Job Aid requries no further refinements, distribute it to your audience.

Make sure to define a metric for the success of the Job Aid, and assess with your audience as it gets used.

For example, are there fewer workplace accidents after the implementation of the job aid?
